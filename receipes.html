Some Osam Coffee Recipes

Capuccino:

Ingredients you Need
  • 2 tablespoons finely ground dark roast coffee
  • 4 ounces water
  • 4 ounces milk
Step 01: Get all items
Step 02: Place the water into the boiler of your espresso machine.
Step 03: Tamp the coffee down, Do this 2 to 3 times.
Step 04: Place the portafilter into your espresso machine's group head and lock it in place.Place a demitasse cup and pull the shot for 23 to 30 seconds.
Step 05: Place the milk into either a glass measuring cup or a small metal pitcher.
Step 06: Insert the steam wand into the container with the milk, just under the surface of the milk.
Step 07: Engage the steam wand on your espresso machine.Make sure to keep the tip of the wand toward the side of the container.
Step 08: Once the milk has foamed to double its size, turn the steam wand off.
Step 09: Top the espresso with foamed milk right after foaming.Cappuccino is ready.

Latte:

Ingredients you Need
  • 1 ounce water
  • 1 tablespoon ground dark espresso or French roast coffee
  • 1 1/2 fluid ounces (or 1 shot) flavored simple syrup, optional
  • 3 ounces milk, or more
Step 01: Get all items
Step 02: Place the water into the boiler of your espresso machine.
Step 03: Tamp the coffee down, Do this 2 to 3 times.
Step 04: Place the portafilter into your espresso machine's group head and lock it in place.Place a demitasse cup and pull the shot for 23 to 30 seconds.
Step 05: Once the shot is pulled, add the flavored syrup, if using.
Step 06: Place the milk into either a glass measuring cup or a small metal pitcher.
Step 07: Engage the steam wand on your espresso machine.Make sure to keep the tip of the wand toward the side of the container. Once the milk has foamed to double its size, turn the steam wand off.
Step 08: Use a spoon to retain the microbubbles on top of the steamed milk and pour the bottom 2/3 of the steamed milk into the latte cup.
Step 09: TTop the latte with the remaining bubbles by pouring or spooning them onto the drink. Serve and enjoy.

Mocha:

Ingredients you Need
  • 2 tablespoons coffee
  • Chocolate
  • 4 ounces milk
  • Whipped cream(optional)
Step 01: Steam the milk: You’ll need hot milk for this recipe. You can opt to steam it or use plain hot milk - more on that below. I recommend steaming it.
Step 02: Brew an espresso or a strong coffee. Use freshly brewed coffee. Coffee will always taste better when using fresh coffee beans to brew. You’ll need 2 ounces (60ml) of espresso/strong coffee.
Step 03: Mix coffee and mocha sauce. Mix the 2 ingredients in the glass or mug you’ll use to prepare your mocha latte. You can now use more mocha sauce to make decorative patterns on your glass cup.
Step 04: Mix steamed milk. Pour in the milk with the espresso chocolate mixture. If the milk was properly steamed, there will be some foam on top of your drink.
Step 05: Top with whipped cream and chocolate syrup. Although totally optional, why not make this drink even more delicious? Top with homemade whipped cream and mocha syrup for the best flavor.

Espresso:

Ingredients you Need
  • 2 tablespoons finely ground dark roast coffee
  • 4 ounces water
Step 01: Use Good Water Fill up your water reservoir. The best is spring water, but distilled water works too.
Step 02: Turn On the Machine Ensure the boiler is hot. The light on your machine will indicate when the machine is ready.
Step 03: Dose your coffee. If your grinder doesn’t have a doser, weighing on a scale is the best option. Use 14-18 grams of coffee beans for a double espresso. Use 7-9 grams for a solo. You can still use volumetric dosing, by measuring the scoops.
Step 04: Grind Coffee Grind your coffee finely. The grind size is smaller than for drip coffee, but not quite a powder. Table salt is a good comparison size.
Step 05: Fill the Portafilter Dump your coffee grounds in the portafilter, aiming to distribute them evenly in the filter basket. Lightly tap the portafilter to achieve perfect distribution.
Step 06: Tamp it. Give it a good tamp, aim for about 30 pounds of pressure. That pressure can vary, depending on your coffee variety and roast, and the grind size.
Step 07: Start Brewing Lock in your portafilter into the brew head of your machine and press the brew button.
Step 08: Pull the shot Aim for a 25 seconds extraction time. Depending on how fine is coffee ground, this can vary up and down by 5 seconds. The extraction time is the same, whether it is a solo or a doppio.

Americano:

Ingredients you Need
  • 10 ounce of hot water
  • Coffee
  • Sugar and stevia
Step 01: Measure your coffee.Pack your espresso machine’s portafilter with 18 g finely and freshly ground espresso beans using a rounded top.
Step 02: Fine Grind the beans
Step 03: Tamp the coffee grounds.Lightly tap the portafilter on the counter to settle the grounds and use a tamping rod to pack the coffee. Use 30 lbs of force to tamp the grounds. Give the tamping rod a slight twist to secure any stray coffee grounds.
Step 04: Put The Portafilter Into The Machine
Step 05: Brew your Espresso.Replace the portafilter and secure the espresso machine. Brew a doppio according to the instructions that came with your espresso machine.
Step 06: Sweeten your drink.Pour the espresso into your drinking cup, adding sweetener if desired. Sugar and stevia are common additions to Americanos.
Step 07: Get into hot water,pour the espresso into the water and enjoy your hot Americano!
